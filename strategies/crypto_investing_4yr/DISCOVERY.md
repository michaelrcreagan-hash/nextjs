## Core Hypothesis

Bitcoin (and by extension crypto broadly) moves in ~4-year cycles tied to the halving. On-chain valuation (MVRV Z-score), sentiment extremes (Fear & Greed Index), and price relative to the 200-week moving average identify cycle-bottom accumulation zones and cycle-top distribution zones. Capital is tranched in during accumulation and scaled out during distribution, rather than timed to a single entry/exit point.

### Market Behavior Exploited
Multi-year boom/bust cycles in crypto driven by halving-cycle supply shocks and reflexive sentiment; on-chain valuation extremes (MVRV) and sentiment extremes (Fear & Greed) have historically marked cycle turning points.

### Theoretical Basis
MVRV Z-score measures market value vs. realized value in standard-deviation terms — extreme low readings have historically coincided with cycle bottoms, extreme high readings with cycle tops. Fear & Greed Index captures sentiment extremes as a contrarian confirmation. 200-week MA is a standard long-term trend/value reference for BTC.

## Distribution Conditions

| Condition | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| MVRV Z-score | above 6.0 |
| Fear & Greed Index | above 85, for ≥ 30 consecutive days |
| Sell rate | 20% of position per month |
| Target allocation during distribution | BTC 45% / ETH 30% / alts 25% |

### Signal Logic
```
accumulation_zone = (price < btc_200wma)
                     and (fear_greed < 25 for >= 30 days)
                     and (btc_dominance > 0.55)
                     and (mvrv_zscore < 1.0)
if accumulation_zone: deploy next 0.25 tranche (of 4)

distribution_zone = (mvrv_zscore > 6.0) and (fear_greed > 85 for >= 30 days)
if distribution_zone: sell 20% of position per month, rebalance toward
                       {btc: 0.45, eth: 0.30, alts: 0.25}
```
